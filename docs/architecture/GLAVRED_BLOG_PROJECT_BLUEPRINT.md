# Главред: быть интересным Project Blueprint

Current as of Slice 2.17.4.3.

This blueprint describes the `project-glavred-blog` benchmark project in Glavred
application terms: project profile, publisher contract, author memory, topics,
fabulas, topic/fabula matrix, channels, signals and ready scenarios. The technical
project id remains `project-glavred-blog`; the public project name is
`Главред: быть интересным`.

## 1. Project Profile

- **Name:** `Главред: быть интересным`.
- **Language:** Russian.
- **Primary channel:** Telegram.
- **Experiment channel:** Dzen for longer explanatory adaptation.
- **Benchmark role:** product philosophy and practical editorial-method benchmark.
- **Core promise:** Glavred helps authors stay interesting with AI instead of
  becoming faster but flatter.

The project is not a generic product-news blog. It is a public workshop about a new
editorial discipline: how to combine author memory, fabulas, source work, model roles,
validation, final quality gate and HITL versions so AI removes routine without erasing
the author.

## 2. Author

The author is the creator and product editor of Glavred. He writes as someone building
an AI-native editorial office in public, not as a vendor listing features.

The author voice should show:

- practical fatigue with flat, averaged AI content;
- respect for human taste, position and final editorial choice;
- systems thinking: memory, context, source handoff, validation and versions;
- willingness to show trade-offs and limitations rather than sell a magic button;
- interest in reusable methods, templates and libraries for real authors.

## 3. Goals

The blog exists to:

- explain the philosophy: `Главред - это быть интересным`;
- attract users and clients who need a managed AI-assisted editorial workflow;
- show that AI can preserve author voice only when surrounded by memory, fabulas,
  sources, checks and human decisions;
- build reusable libraries and templates for marketers, designers, consultants,
  founders and expert authors;
- prepare the future Telegram + Dzen multi-platform benchmark without losing the
  same author position.

## 4. Audience

Primary readers:

- experts and consultants who know a lot but struggle to publish regularly;
- founders and small B2B teams that need founder-led or expert-led content;
- marketers and product marketing teams that want author position, not content sludge;
- designers and product people who need to explain practical thinking publicly;
- editors and agencies who want to use AI as an assistant, not as a replacement for
  editorial judgment.

Their core pain:

> AI writes quickly, but the result is often smooth, safe and boring. Writing manually
> is expensive. Prompt games consume time. Being interesting remains hard.

## 5. Positioning

Glavred is not an autoposter. It is an editorial system.

The product does not claim that a user can enter a blog title and receive an endless
stream of unique, charismatic posts. It says something stricter and more useful:

- AI can bring material, drafts, variants, checks and structure;
- the author still owns position, taste, final choice and public responsibility;
- interest is produced by a system: author memory, topic, fabula, evidence, validators,
  final gate and HITL;
- the system should make routine cheaper and quality more visible, not hide work
  behind a single prompt.

## 6. Style

Style should feel like a product-builder's editorial workshop:

- direct, clear, practical and slightly sharp;
- starts from recognizable editorial pain;
- avoids generic AI-copywriting advice;
- explains internals through reader benefit;
- gives a usable method, template, criterion or diagnostic question;
- translates internal terms into human value.

Allowed internal terms:

- `память автора`;
- `фабула`;
- `версии`;
- `финальная проверка`;
- `HITL`;
- `источники`;
- `контекст`.

Technical names such as `SourceLedger`, `finalQualityGate`, `revisionLoop` and
`ContextPack` may appear only when immediately explained as a practical editorial
mechanism.

## 7. Forbidden Moves

- Product feature announcements without an editorial pain.
- "AI magic" or "one prompt solves content" framing.
- Internal trace dumps as public prose.
- Generic "10 tips for AI content" posts.
- Templates that do not show when and how to adapt them.
- Treating the latest machine revision as automatically better.

## 8. Author Memory

Seeded author-memory themes:

- `Главред - это быть интересным`: speed is not enough; the author must keep nerve,
  position and public usefulness.
- AI should "bring the shells": collect material, produce options and run checks,
  but not command the author.
- Prompting alone is not an editorial system.
- Templates are useful only as living role-specific workpieces.
- The final version is a human decision; the last generated version is not always
  the best one.

## 9. Editorial Rules

Publisher blocks must be backed by active `editorialRules`:

- `author`: author as product editor and builder of an editorial workshop;
- `audience`: write for people who must sound interesting regularly;
- `goal`: prove that AI can help an author be interesting, and build practical
  template libraries;
- `positioning`: no magic autoposter promise;
- `styleVoice`: start from recognizable editorial pain;
- `styleLanguage`: translate internal terms into author/editor benefit;
- `antiAiPattern`: do not turn the blog into feature-release notes.

## 10. Topics

Topics are territories, not post genres.

1. **Быть интересным**
   Why AI texts become smooth and boring, and how to restore tension, position and
   reader value.

2. **Редакционная машина**
   How author memory, fabulas, sources, validation, final gate, versions and decision
   snapshot work as one system.

3. **AI без обезличивания**
   How to use models without flattening the author's voice.

4. **Библиотеки и шаблоны**
   Role-specific reusable templates for marketer, designer, consultant, founder and
   expert author.

5. **Разборы до/после**
   Why one draft is dead and another feels alive; what exactly changed.

6. **Практика внедрения**
   Who Glavred helps, where it does not, and how to make it part of a real workflow.

## 11. Fabulas

Fabulas are reusable story mechanics.

1. **Плоский черновик**
   AI draft -> why it is dead -> what returns the author -> revised text -> rule.

2. **Редакционный прием**
   Pain -> one method -> example -> how to apply -> where it fails.

3. **Как устроено внутри**
   Author pain -> why one prompt fails -> Glavred mechanism -> editor benefit.

4. **Шаблон для роли**
   Role -> common pain -> template -> adaptation guide -> first example.

5. **До / после**
   Source material -> machine draft -> editor decision -> final version -> lesson.

6. **Анти-паттерн**
   Tempting AI shortcut -> why people use it -> failure mode -> better replacement.

## 12. Topic/Fabula Matrix

The matrix is curated, not one-to-one:

- `Плоский черновик`: `Быть интересным`, `Разборы до/после`, `AI без обезличивания`;
- `Редакционный прием`: `Быть интересным`, `Редакционная машина`, `Библиотеки и шаблоны`;
- `Как устроено внутри`: `Редакционная машина`, `AI без обезличивания`, `Практика внедрения`;
- `Шаблон для роли`: `Библиотеки и шаблоны`, `Практика внедрения`, `Быть интересным`;
- `До / после`: `Разборы до/после`, `AI без обезличивания`, `Редакционная машина`;
- `Анти-паттерн`: `Быть интересным`, `Библиотеки и шаблоны`, `Практика внедрения`.

Each fabula applies to at least three topics, so it remains a reusable dramaturgy
rather than a renamed topic.

## 13. Channels

Channels own destination mechanics only.

- `Telegram`: primary, short practical product-philosophy notes.
- `Dzen`: experiment, longer explainers and template-library articles.

Canonical audience stays in the publisher `audience` rules, not in `PublicationChannel`.

## 14. Source Signals

Seeded signals:

- `AI пишет быстро, но слишком часто получается гладко и неинтересно`;
- `Память автора нужна, чтобы модель не каждый раз начинала с нуля`;
- `Маркетологу, дизайнеру и консультанту нужны разные редакционные заготовки`;
- `Редактор может выбрать не последнюю версию`.

## 15. Ready Scenario

Ready plan slot:

- **Title:** `Почему AI пишет гладко, но неинтересно`.
- **Topic:** `Быть интересным`.
- **Fabula:** `Плоский черновик`.
- **Channel:** Telegram.
- **Expected effect:** explain why one prompt does not preserve author voice, how the
  editorial system returns interestingness, and what work still belongs to the human.

## 16. Benchmark Criteria

A good generated post should:

- start from the real pain of flat AI content;
- make the philosophy `Главред - это быть интересным` explicit;
- show Glavred as an editorial system, not a magic writer;
- preserve author voice and human decision;
- include one practical takeaway, template, criterion or next action;
- avoid generic product marketing and internal artifact dumps.
